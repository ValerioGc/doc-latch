Open-source desktop app

Read and protect your PDFs, without the cloud

DocLatch is a lightweight PDF viewer for Windows, Linux and macOS. Open several documents in tabs, navigate with smooth zoom, and add or remove password protection — everything runs on your own computer, never online.

What you can do

Key features

Multi-document tabs

Open several PDFs at once in separate tabs, each keeping its own current page, zoom level and password.

Password protection

Add or remove AES-256 encryption from your documents in a couple of clicks, right from the Protection menu.

Smooth zoom and scrolling

Navigate pages with smooth zooming: a loading indicator appears while the view is recalculated, never a blurry or clipped page.

Recent documents

Quickly find recently opened files from the home screen, and remove them from the list whenever you no longer need them.

Light and dark theme

Switch between light and dark themes at any time from the settings, to match your preference.

Multilingual interface

Available in Italian, English, French and German, with the language switchable from settings.

Getting started

Quick guides

Open and navigate a document

  1. Launch DocLatch and choose "Open file…" from the File menu, or pick a document from the recent list on the home screen.
  2. Use the arrow keys, Page Up/Down, or the mouse wheel to scroll through pages.
  3. Adjust the zoom from the status bar, or with Ctrl/Cmd and +/-/0 on the keyboard.
  4. Open a second document to see it in a new tab, without losing the first one.

Protect a document with a password

  1. With the document open, go to Protection > Add password… in the top menu.
  2. Enter the chosen password: the file is encrypted with AES-256 and saved as a new copy.
  3. To remove protection later, use Protection > Remove password… and enter the current password.
  4. Protection > Security info… lets you check the encryption status of the open document at any time.

Compatibility

Supported formats

PDF

DocLatch currently handles PDF files only. Exporting to other formats is on the project's future roadmap.

Privacy

Your documents stay on your computer

DocLatch never uploads your files to any server: opening, browsing and password-protecting documents all happen entirely locally, with no internet connection and no usage data collection.

Windows SmartScreen notice

The Windows and macOS installers aren't signed with a paid certificate yet, so it's normal for SmartScreen (or Gatekeeper on macOS) to show a warning on first launch. You can proceed by choosing "More info" and then "Run anyway".
